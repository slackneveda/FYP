# Professional DOCX Thesis Formatting Guide

## Document Setup for Microsoft Word

### 1. Page Layout Settings
- **Paper Size**: A4 (21.0 cm Ã— 29.7 cm)
- **Margins**: 
  - Top: 2.5 cm
  - Bottom: 2.5 cm
  - Left: 3.0 cm (for binding)
  - Right: 2.0 cm
- **Orientation**: Portrait
- **Multiple Pages**: Mirror Margins (for binding)

### 2. Font Formatting
- **Main Text**: Times New Roman, 12 pt
- **Headings**: Times New Roman, Bold
  - Chapter Titles: 16 pt, Center-aligned
  - Section Headings: 14 pt, Left-aligned
  - Subsection Headings: 12 pt, Bold, Left-aligned
- **Captions**: Times New Roman, 10 pt
- **Footnotes**: Times New Roman, 10 pt
- **Table of Contents**: Times New Roman, 11 pt

### 3. Line Spacing and Paragraphs
- **Main Text**: 1.5 line spacing
- **Block Quotes**: Single line spacing, indented 1.27 cm from both margins
- **References**: Single line spacing
- **Paragraph Spacing**: 
  - Before: 6 pt
  - After: 6 pt
- **First Line Indent**: 1.27 cm for all paragraphs except headings

### 4. Title Page Formatting

#### University Header
```
THE UNIVERSITY OF LAHORE
DEPARTMENT OF TECHNOLOGY
```
- Font: Times New Roman, 14 pt, BOLD, CENTERED, ALL CAPS
- Position: Top margin (3.0 cm from top)

#### Thesis Title
```
Sweet Dessert: AI-Powered E-Commerce Platform for Premium Desserts
```
- Font: Times New Roman, 16 pt, BOLD, CENTERED
- Position: Center of page
- Spacing: Double line spacing

#### Degree Information
```
A dissertation submitted in partial fulfillment of the requirements
for the degree of Bachelor of Science in Computer Science
```
- Font: Times New Roman, 12 pt, CENTERED
- Position: Below title

#### Student Information
```
Submitted by:
Student Name 1 (BSMET-2021-001)
Student Name 2 (BSMET-2021-002)
Student Name 3 (BSMET-2021-003)
```
- Font: Times New Roman, 12 pt, CENTERED
- Position: Below degree information

#### Supervisor Information
```
Supervised by:
Dr. A B C
Professor, Department of Technology

Co-Supervisor:
Dr. X Y Z
Assistant Professor, Department of Technology
```
- Font: Times New Roman, 12 pt, CENTERED
- Position: Below student information

#### Date
```
Fall/Winter, 2025
```
- Font: Times New Roman, 12 pt, CENTERED
- Position: Bottom third of page

### 5. Chapter Formatting

#### Chapter Numbers and Titles
```
CHAPTER 1

INTRODUCTION
```
- Chapter Number: Times New Roman, 16 pt, BOLD, CENTERED
- Chapter Title: Times New Roman, 16 pt, BOLD, CENTERED, ALL CAPS
- Spacing: 24 pt before, 24 pt after

#### Section Headings
```
1.1 Background and Motivation
```
- Font: Times New Roman, 14 pt, BOLD, LEFT-aligned
- Numbering: Decimal system (1.1, 1.2, etc.)
- Spacing: 12 pt before, 6 pt after

#### Subsection Headings
```
1.1.1 Research Context
```
- Font: Times New Roman, 12 pt, BOLD, LEFT-aligned
- Numbering: Decimal system (1.1.1, 1.1.2, etc.)
- Spacing: 6 pt before, 3 pt after

### 6. Table of Contents Formatting
- **Title**: "TABLE OF CONTENTS" - Times New Roman, 16 pt, BOLD, CENTERED
- **Chapter Entries**: Times New Roman, 11 pt, BOLD
- **Section Entries**: Times New Roman, 11 pt
- **Leader Dots**: Use dot leaders (............)
- **Page Numbers**: Right-aligned, Times New Roman, 11 pt
- **Spacing**: Single line spacing between entries

### 7. Tables and Figures

#### Table Formatting
- **Table Caption**: "Table 1.1: Description" - Times New Roman, 10 pt, BOLD, CENTERED, above table
- **Table Content**: Times New Roman, 10 pt
- **Alignment**: Center-aligned horizontally
- **Borders**: Top and bottom borders, no internal borders
- **Spacing**: 6 pt before and after

#### Figure Formatting
- **Figure Caption**: "Figure 1.1: Description" - Times New Roman, 10 pt, CENTERED, below figure
- **Figure Position**: Centered on page
- **Spacing**: 6 pt before and after

### 8. Page Numbering
- **Preliminary Pages**: Roman numerals (i, ii, iii, iv, v, vi)
  - Title Page: No page number
  - Declaration: Page ii
  - Preface: Page iii
  - Acknowledgements: Page iv
  - Abstract: Page v
  - Table of Contents: Page vi
- **Main Body**: Arabic numerals (1, 2, 3, ...)
  - Start from Chapter 1: Page 1
  - Bottom center or bottom right alignment

### 9. References Formatting
- **Title**: "REFERENCES" - Times New Roman, 16 pt, BOLD, CENTERED
- **Entries**: Times New Roman, 11 pt, Hanging indent (1.27 cm)
- **Spacing**: Single line spacing, 6 pt between entries
- **Format**: APA or IEEE style (consistent throughout)

### 10. Appendices Formatting
- **Title**: "APPENDICES" - Times New Roman, 16 pt, BOLD, CENTERED
- **Appendix Letters**: APPENDIX A, APPENDIX B, etc.
- **Appendix Titles**: Times New Roman, 14 pt, BOLD, CENTERED

## Conversion Steps from Markdown to DOCX

### Step 1: Document Setup
1. Open Microsoft Word
2. Set page layout to A4 with specified margins
3. Configure font styles for different elements
4. Set up paragraph styles for consistency

### Step 2: Content Transfer
1. Copy content from Markdown file
2. Paste into Word document
3. Apply formatting according to style guide
4. Ensure proper heading hierarchy

### Step 3: Professional Touches
1. Add page numbers in correct format
2. Generate automatic Table of Contents
3. Add section breaks between chapters
4. Insert proper page breaks
5. Check widow/orphan control

### Step 4: Final Review
1. Check consistency of formatting
2. Verify all headings are properly numbered
3. Ensure all tables and figures are captioned
4. Check spelling and grammar
5. Verify page numbering sequence

## Tips for Professional Appearance

### Consistency
- Use consistent formatting throughout
- Apply styles rather than manual formatting
- Ensure uniform spacing and alignment

### Readability
- Use appropriate font sizes for different elements
- Maintain adequate white space
- Avoid overcrowding text

### Academic Standards
- Follow university guidelines strictly
- Include all required sections
- Ensure proper citation format
- Maintain formal academic tone

### Technical Considerations
- Use Word's built-in heading styles for TOC generation
- Apply paragraph styles for consistency
- Use section breaks for chapter separation
- Enable widow/orphan control for professional appearance

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Inconsistent Formatting**: Different fonts or sizes for similar elements
2. **Incorrect Margins**: Not following university specifications
3. **Poor Spacing**: Too much or too little space between elements
4. **Missing Elements**: Forgetting page numbers or proper captions
5. **Incorrect Numbering**: Inconsistent heading or page numbering
6. **Alignment Issues**: Not properly centering or aligning elements

This formatting guide will help you create a professional, university-standard thesis document from your Markdown report.
